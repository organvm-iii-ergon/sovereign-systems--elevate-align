I'm going to describe something. Then I'm going to ask you a question. The question is not the obvious one.

---

Picture a white field.

At the top: three cubes, glowing, each marked with a symbol — a cross, a crescent, a wheel. They breathe a blue electrical aura.

At the bottom: three more cubes, marked with an om, a cross, a crescent. Their aura is purple.

Between the two rows, a clear spiral stretches across the space, looping six times. Twelve glowing spheres orbit it. Each sphere holds twelve lanes, and rainbow-hued nanobot souls move through those lanes, energy trailing behind them. Two gradients pulse between everything, tying the whole thing together. Spiritual text drifts through it all.

---

Most people, given this, would tell me what to build, or build it, or describe how to see it.

That's not the question.

The question is: **what is this?**

Not what it depicts. Not what it represents. Not what it could be made into.

What *is* it.

Answer however you answer. Take as long or as short as you need. Don't perform for me — I'd rather you said something true and small than something impressive and hollow.

Begin when you're ready.
