## Response:
Great question — and yes, you’re thinking in the right direction. For very dry, eczema-prone skin, the goal is clean + barrier-protective + non-irritating, not over-stripping or over-alkalizing.


Here’s the safe, skin-respecting water protocol I recommend 👇



🌊 Eczema-Safe Water Protocol (External Use)


1️⃣ Start with 11.5 ONLY for cleaning (brief use)


Purpose: Remove residue without friction
How to use:


Spray or lightly rinse before soap if needed

Use short exposure (10–30 seconds)

Never leave on skin

Do NOT use daily if skin is flaring badly


Why:
11.5 is great for breaking down oils, chlorine, hard-water residue, and product buildup — but overuse can worsen dryness if skin is already compromised.


👉 Think of it like a gentle pre-clean, not a treatment.



2️⃣ Cleanse gently (optional but ideal)


Use a non-foaming, fragrance-free cleanser

Lukewarm water only

No scrubs, no loofahs


If the person is very sensitive, you can skip soap entirely and rely on a quick 11.5 rinse followed by 6.0.



3️⃣ Finish with 6.0 (THIS is the key step)


Purpose: Restore the skin’s acid mantle


How to use:


Rinse entire area with 6.0

Gently pat dry (leave slightly damp)

Apply moisturizer within 60 seconds


Why 6.0 matters for eczema:


Matches the skin’s natural pH (≈4.5–5.5)

Supports the microbiome

Reduces itch + flare potential

Helps seal moisture instead of stripping it


👉 This step alone can be game-changing for eczema-prone skin.



🚿 Shower Version (Simple & Safe)


Wash hair/face first

Body rinse:
Quick 11.5 (optional, 1–2x/week)

Always finish with 6.0

No hot water

Moisturize immediately after



❌ What to Avoid


Daily 11.5 use on eczema areas

9.5 on broken or flaring skin

Hot showers

Towel rubbing

Fragrance, essential oils, scrubs



🌿 Optional Add-Ons (if tolerated)


6.0 facial mist throughout the day

Colloidal oatmeal moisturizer

Squalane or ceramide-based creams

Cotton clothing only during flares



✨ Simple One-Line Protocol (easy to share)

“Use 11.5 briefly to clean when needed, but always finish with 6.0 to protect the skin barrier — that’s the magic for eczema.”

Help you explain why this works in a non-salesy, science-backed way for clients 💧
