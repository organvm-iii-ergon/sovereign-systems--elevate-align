Love this goal—classic hourglass powerhouse vibe! As a fitness coach, you’ll want to approach this with a smart combo of hypertrophy training, muscle balance, and nutrition/hormone support. Here's a breakdown to get you that “bubble butt + toned legs + flat stomach + small waist” look:



1. Glute-Focused Hypertrophy Training (2–3x/week)


Your glutes need volume, progressive overload, and rest.


Core Lifts:


Hip thrusts (heavy & high-rep days)

Bulgarian split squats (glute-biased, torso leaning forward)

Romanian deadlifts (for glute-hamstring tie-in)

Cable kickbacks / abduction work (high rep, accessory)


Sets/Reps:


Mix 8–12 reps (growth) and 12–20 reps (endurance/pump)

Train 2–3 times/week with at least 48 hrs between heavy glute days



2. Legs: Toned, Not Bulky


Keep legs strong, but prioritize glute-dominant compound movements and lighter quad work if you build size easily.


Tips:


Focus on time under tension and mind-muscle connection

Use unilateral moves (split squats, lunges) to keep symmetry

Avoid heavy squats or leg presses if they blow up your quads—opt for glute bridges or step-ups



3. Waist: Small + Stomach Flat


Here’s where nutrition, posture, and deep core work shine.


Training Tips:


Vacuum breathing / transverse abdominis activation (think: pull navel to spine)

Pilates-based core workouts (planks, dead bugs, bird dogs—not heavy oblique twisting)

Avoid weighted side bends that can thicken your waist


Nutrition:


Stay lean with a slight calorie surplus for glute growth

Focus on anti-inflammatory foods to avoid bloating

Cycle carbs around leg/glute days



4. Cardio & Hormones


Use low-impact cardio (like incline walking or cycling) for fat-burning and glute retention

High cortisol = belly fat, so avoid chronic high-intensity workouts that spike stress

Keep estrogen/progesterone balanced if aiming for a naturally cinched waist
