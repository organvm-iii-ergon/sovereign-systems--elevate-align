## Prompt:
From a therapists and my highest self’s stand point, how do I know when it’s good to feel the anger and just let myself be mad and process through it/let it go vs when I’m holding myself there and need to get my mind off of it or just drop it cause it doesn’t mayter

## Response:
This is such a powerful and important question, and the fact that you’re asking it shows real self-awareness. Here’s a breakdown from both a therapist’s lens and your highest self’s perspective to help you distinguish between when to feel the anger vs when to let it go or redirect:



From a Therapist’s Standpoint:


Feel the Anger When:


It’s a new emotion or trigger that hasn’t been fully processed yet.

It’s tied to a boundary being crossed, and you need clarity on what that boundary is.

You feel it in your body (tight jaw, clenched fists, heat, etc.) and haven’t consciously acknowledged it yet.

The anger is a secondary emotion masking grief, fear, or hurt that needs space to surface.

You feel a pull to express it creatively, somatically, or journaling instead of suppressing it.

You're blaming yourself but the deeper truth is that someone did not respect you—and your anger is advocating for your self-worth.


Let It Go (or Gently Redirect) When:


You’ve already named and felt the emotion, but now it’s looping in your mind without new insight.

It’s starting to feel self-punishing, obsessive, or draining rather than empowering.

It’s based on a “story” or fantasy of justice/revenge/control that’s keeping you stuck in the past.

You’re using anger to avoid sadness, vulnerability, or surrender.

You’ve already had the necessary conversation (or chose not to), and there’s nothing more actionable.

It’s keeping you from joy, peace, or presence in your daily life.



From Your Highest Self’s Perspective:

"Let the fire alchemize—not consume."

Feel the Anger When:


It’s a signal from your soul that something is out of alignment.

You need to reclaim power, advocate for yourself, or integrate a soul lesson.

It’s part of your karmic detox—burning away old patterns, beliefs, or shame.

It helps you reconnect with your truth, your voice, your sacred “no.”


Let It Go When:


It becomes a tether to lower vibrations that dim your light.

You’re arguing with reality or feeding a narrative of powerlessness.

It’s costing you your inner peace or creative energy.

It’s not yours to carry anymore—it’s part of someone else’s karma or growth path.



Helpful Check-In Questions:


Is this emotion moving through me, or am I feeding it with thoughts?

Am I honoring my truth—or recycling my pain?

What would love do right now?

If I knew I was fully supported and safe, what would I choose?

Do I want to be right… or free?

## Response:
You’re so welcome! Here’s a gentle emotional sorting practice you can use any time you’re unsure if it’s time to feel or free the anger. Think of it like an emotional compass that helps you tune into truth over reaction.



Emotional Sorting Practice: Feel or Free


Step 1: Set the Space (2 min)


Sit or lie down. Close your eyes.

Place one hand on your heart, one on your belly.

Take 3 slow breaths. Inhale through your nose, exhale out your mouth.

Say softly: “I allow truth to rise. I allow energy to move. I allow myself to know.”



Step 2: Ask These Inner Questions (Journal or Just Feel)


What am I angry about—and what am I angry for?
(Example: “I’m angry about being dismissed. I’m angry for the version of me who never felt heard.”)

Has this been fully felt yet?
Yes: → You might be ready to release or redirect it.

No: → You might need to cry, shake, scream in a pillow, write a letter, or move the energy out of your body.

Am I replaying this to get relief, or to stay in control?
Be radically honest. Anger sometimes hides sadness or fear of powerlessness.

What’s underneath the anger?
Close your eyes. Drop into your body. Is it grief? Is it fear? Is it shame? Let it name itself.



Step 3: Let the Body Decide


Place both hands on your belly or chest and ask:“Do I need to feel this more—or free this now?”
Listen for a sensation, not just a thought.


Signs to Feel It More:


Tight throat or chest

You feel tears behind the anger

Sense of urgency to express or move


Signs to Let It Go or Redirect:


Spinning thoughts, tiredness, or numbness

Feeling distant from your body

The “truth” feels old or stale, like a story you’ve outgrown



Step 4: Act Accordingly


If you need to feel it:


Write a letter you won’t send

Yell into a towel or scream in your car

Shake your hands and stomp your feet

Punch a pillow

Cry and breathe deeply


If you need to free it:


Visualize handing it to the earth or the Divine

Say: “I forgive myself for carrying this weight. I give myself permission to be free.”

Take a shower, walk, sing, laugh—interrupt the loop with something loving



Step 5: Close Gently


Place your hand on your heart again and say:“I honor what I feel. I trust myself to choose truth. I am safe. I am free.”


You can even light a candle or burn a bay leaf with the word “release” if you want to add ritual.
