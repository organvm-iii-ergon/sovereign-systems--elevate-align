## Prompt:
How to take my pdf and get it on my kindle so I can read it like that and scroll instead of pages with tiny words

## Response:
Totally doable — and way easier than Amazon makes it seem. There are two best ways depending on how you want the PDF to behave on your Kindle:



⭐️ If you want it to reflow like a book (bigger text, scroll, not tiny page boxes)


Use the Send-to-Kindle feature with conversion.


1️⃣ Find your Kindle email address


Open the Amazon Kindle app

Go to Settings → Send to Kindle

You’ll see an email like: yourname_123@kindle.com


2️⃣ Email the PDF to that address with the word convert in the subject


Example email:


To: yourname@kindle.com  
Subject: convert  
Attachment: yourfile.pdf


✦ This tells Amazon to convert the PDF into Kindle format, so you can:


Change font size

Highlight

Scroll or page normally

Read it like a Kindle book



⭐️ If you want to keep it as-is but bigger and scrollable


Use the Send to Kindle app:


1️⃣ Download the app:


Search: Send to Kindle (Mac or PC)


2️⃣ Open it → drag the PDF into it → choose your Kindle → send.


Then on your Kindle:


Open file

Tap Aa (font button)

Choose Fit to Screen or Continuous Scrolling if supported.


(Some older Kindles don’t scroll PDFs, but the app tries to optimize them.)



⭐️ Extra Tip: Use Kindle’s web uploader


Go here (on your phone or laptop):
➡️ https://send.amazon.com/document/upload


Upload your PDF → choose device → send.


Then tap Convert to Kindle Format if you want bigger text & scrolling.



📍 On the Kindle


Once the PDF arrives:


Go to Library

Tap the three dots next to the file (if visible)

Select "Convert to Kindle format" if you didn’t do the email trick.
