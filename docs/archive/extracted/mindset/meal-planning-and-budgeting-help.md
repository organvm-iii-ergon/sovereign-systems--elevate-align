Great list! Here are some suggestions to help you make the most out of your staples while incorporating more veggies and fruits into your meals:


Plan for Veggies:


Buy Frozen Veggies: They last longer, and you can add them to almost any meal—stir-fries, soups, rice bowls, and burritos.

Prep Fresh Veggies: When you buy fresh veggies, chop them right away and store them in the fridge for easy use in multiple meals.


Meal Ideas:


1. Fried Rice


Ingredients: Rice, eggs, frozen/fresh veggies (like carrots, peas, bell peppers), soy sauce, and whatever protein you have (leftover meats or tofu).

Pro Tip: Make extra rice when cooking other meals to repurpose for fried rice. Use veggies that are nearing their expiry to avoid waste.


2. Breakfast Burritos


Ingredients: Eggs, potatoes (roasted or pan-fried), veggies (peppers, onions, spinach), cottage cheese or regular cheese, and wraps.

Pro Tip: Batch cook filling, then freeze burritos for quick meals.


3. Grilled Cheese with Veggies


Ingredients: Bread, cheese, veggies (like spinach or tomatoes), and optional protein.

Pro Tip: Serve with a simple veggie soup or salad on the side to use up fresh veggies.


4. Soups


Ingredients: Potatoes, quinoa, veggies (carrots, onions, celery), and broth.

Pro Tip: Make a large pot and freeze portions. Use up any veggies on the verge of going bad.


5. Nachos


Ingredients: Tortilla chips, cheese, beans, salsa, and diced veggies like peppers, tomatoes, or spinach.

Pro Tip: Add extra veggies to the toppings or sauté them for added flavor.


6. Stir-Fries


Ingredients: Rice or quinoa, veggies (carrots, broccoli, bell peppers), and a sauce like teriyaki or soy.

Pro Tip: Use frozen veggies or any leftover fresh veggies before they spoil.


7. Rice Bowls


Ingredients: Rice, veggies (roasted or steamed), eggs, and a dressing or sauce.

Pro Tip: Change it up by using quinoa or potatoes as the base. Great for mixing in fruits like avocado.


Easy Veggie & Fruit Incorporation:


Add Veggies to Breakfast: Throw spinach, bell peppers, or onions into scrambled eggs or breakfast burritos.

Smoothies: Use yogurt, orange juice, and fruit for quick, healthy snacks or breakfasts. Throw in spinach or kale for an extra veggie boost.

Sheet Pan Veggies: Roast a large batch of veggies (carrots, broccoli, sweet potatoes) and use them throughout the week in rice bowls, burritos, and side dishes.


Would these ideas help? We can adjust the meal plans further based on how often you want to cook or freeze things!
