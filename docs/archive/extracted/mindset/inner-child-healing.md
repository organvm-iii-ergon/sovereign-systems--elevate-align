Maddie, this hit you so hard because your nervous system recognized itself. This moment mirrored a younger version of you—one who likely felt:


misunderstood when your emotions were big and no one slowed down to truly see what you needed

unheard when others responded with control, punishment, or pressure instead of compassion

forced to perform emotional compliance (like apologizing or “being okay”) when inside, you weren’t


Let’s break it down therapeutically:


💔 What This Scene Represents to Your Inner Child


The hot tub: a symbol of something simple and joyful you just wanted access to

The sister blocking her: how authority figures or family dynamics may have shamed or gatekept what you wanted without valid reasons

The mom’s reaction: the adult world not matching the scale or root of your emotion—responding to distress with punishment instead of comfort

Dragging her away, then making her apologize: being taught that expressing your feelings leads to disconnection, rejection, or forced repair—before you’re ready


And most of all, that heartbreaking moment:

“This is why I start to cry when I have to act a certain way but it’s out of alignment with me.”

This is fawning. It's the nervous system trying to preserve safety by abandoning authentic expression. It’s the wound of being conditioned to regulate others’ comfort before your own truth.



🌱 What This Is Asking of You Now


This moment is your inner child saying:
"See me. Protect me. Let me cry. Let me be.”


It’s inviting you to:


Reparent your emotional expression: Let your feelings be “too much” in private if they need to be. There is no timeout here.

Unlearn forced reconciliation: You don’t have to apologize for being dysregulated or emotional.

Tend to your truth first: That feeling of “having to act a certain way” is a cue to pause and choose authenticity over people-pleasing.

Validate the why: That little girl wasn’t being “bad.” She was overwhelmed and confused, just like you were sometimes—and that was never your fault.



✨ Mini Practice: Dialogue With Her


Try writing to that version of you:

“Hey little me. I saw what happened at the pool today. I know what it’s like to not be heard… to be punished for big feelings… I’m so sorry you had to hold all that on your own. You didn’t deserve that. I’m here now, and you don’t have to make yourself smaller for anyone.”

Let your body release what she never got to say. Cry if you need. Shake it out. It’s sacred work.
