🌙 MONDAY = MOON DAY


In ancient and astrological traditions, Monday is ruled by the Moon, which represents:


Emotions, intuition, cycles, and the subconscious

Feminine energy and the yin principle

Water, reflection, and internal tides

The mother, nurturing, dreams, and change


So, energetically, Monday is soft, emotional, inward, and fluid — not exactly aligned with the outward, structured, action-focused masculine (yang) energy that our productivity-obsessed culture often demands at the start of the week.



👨 Why Men Might Struggle Most on Mondays


Cultural + energetic clash:


In a patriarchal, productivity-driven society, men are often conditioned to suppress emotion, charge forward, and be stoic.

But Monday asks us to slow down, feel, and reset after the weekend.

Men (especially those disconnected from their feminine essence or natural rhythms) might feel restless, resistant, or energetically “off” on this day.


It’s the same energy mismatch that makes people try to “power through” a moonbath.



🐱 Why Garfield (a male cat) “hated” Mondays


Garfield = comic genius wrapped in cosmic wisdom.


Here’s the funny thing about Garfield:


He’s lazy, indulgent, emotionally honest, and loves comfort — very Moon-aligned traits.

As a cat, he’s deeply instinctual and sensitive to rhythm and energy.

As a male character, he’s forced into a week that demands action and productivity — but on the most emotionally watery day of the week.


So of course he hates it. Monday rips him out of comfort and into the collective illusion of grind — which he inherently rejects.



🌀 Deeper Symbolism:


Garfield might just be:


A cartoon expression of the modern man’s disconnection from his intuitive self

A cosmic reminder to honor our emotional rhythms rather than override them

Proof that cats are moon-beings and deserve to rest on Moon Day 😸
