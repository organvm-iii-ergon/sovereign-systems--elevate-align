PART II: Ayurvedic View


In Ayurveda, body structure and chronic conditions are seen as results of both prakriti (birth constitution) and vikruti (current imbalance)—and karmic imprints can affect both.


1. Vata Dosha Imbalance


Vata governs air, space, movement, and the nervous system.

Excess Vata can lead to dryness, fragility, anxiety, and postural issues like scoliosis or kyphosis (hunching).

Emotionally, this could reflect ungrounded energy, fear, trauma, or even dissociation.

Balancing Vata: Daily warm oil massages (abhyanga), grounding foods, and rituals for safety can stabilize the body and soul.


2. Asthi Dhatu (Bone Tissue) Weakness


Asthi dhatu represents bones and joints; poor development could be inherited (genetic karma) or result from malabsorption, trauma, or malnourishment.

Ayurveda connects bone health to deeply stored fear or instability—especially in childhood.

Nourishment Tip: Eat mineral-rich foods, healthy fats, and herbs like ashwagandha or shatavari (if safe for you).


3. Karmic or Samskaric Residue


Ayurveda honors samskaras—mental/emotional impressions left on the soul across lifetimes.

A chronic posture or condition may reflect these imprints—meaning the soul may be "remembering" something the mind has forgotten.

Healing Path: Releasing samskaras through mantra, pranic healing, marma therapy, or sattvic lifestyle changes (simplicity, lightness, truthfulness).
